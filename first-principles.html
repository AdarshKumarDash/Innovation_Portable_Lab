First Principles Thinking

Applied to the Portable Lab Innovation

1. Core Problem in Science Education PROBLEM

• Many schools lack functional science laboratories

• Existing labs are expensive and infrastructure-heavy

• Practical learning is limited to theory

Portable Lab addresses the gap between theory and hands-on learning.

2. Goal of the Portable Lab GOAL

• Enable hands-on experiments anywhere

• Reduce dependency on fixed lab infrastructure

• Keep the system affordable and safe

Goal: Practical science at every desk, not just in labs.

3. Challenging Traditional Lab Assumptions ASSUMPTIONS

• A laboratory must be a dedicated room ❌

• Equipment must be large and costly ❌

• Experiments require heavy power supply ❌

Portable Lab removes size, cost, and location assumptions.

4. Fundamental Requirements of a Lab FACTS

• Every experiment needs measurement

• Observations must be visible and repeatable

• Safety is non-negotiable

Measurement Observation Accuracy Calibration Control Data Power Safety Repeatability Reliability Portability
5. Constraints vs Beliefs (Portable Lab) FILTER

• Limited space → belief

• High cost → belief

• Student safety → real constraint

Portable Lab optimizes design while respecting safety.

6. Rebuilding the Lab from First Principles DESIGN

• Replace bulky machines with sensors

• Use ESP32 / microcontroller for versatility

• Make modules interchangeable

Sensors + ESP32 + battery + modular experiment units

7. Testing & Classroom Validation TEST

• Can students use it independently? ✔️

• Does it give accurate results? ✔️

• Can it fit into school curriculum? ✔️

8. Innovation Impact of the Portable Lab IMPACT

• Affordable science education

• Accessible to rural & low-budget schools

• Scalable from Lab 1.0 to Lab 2.0

Portable Lab transforms laboratories into learning products.
